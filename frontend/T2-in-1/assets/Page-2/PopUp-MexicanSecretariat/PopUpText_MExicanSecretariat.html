MEXICAN SECRETARIAT OF AGRICULTURE AND DEVELOPMENT

The Mexican Secretariat of Agriculture and Development, (now the Secretariat of Agriculture, Livestock, Rural Development, Fisheries and Food, or SAGARPA) was created in 1917 as part of the reforms resulting from the Mexican Revolution. It is responsible for administering federal resources to rural development, promotes research for rural purposes, regulates fishing activities and controls all medicines and food for domestic and farm animals.
