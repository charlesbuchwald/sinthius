3. Colombia @CIAT

1966-Current. Focus on Maize

Currently active

CIMMYT’s collaboration with Columbia began in 1966. The International Center for Tropical Agriculture (CIAT) later partnered with CIMMYT to assist in certain aspects of seed production for the Andean Regional Maize Program. This regional cooperative program works intensively to assist the national programs of the region (Bolivia, Brazil, Colombia, Ecuador, Paraguay, Peru, and Venezuela) in improving maize research and production.

6. Iran<

1966-Current. Focus on Wheat

Currently active

CIMMYT has collaborated with the Islamic Republic of Iran since the 1960’s. Today, more than 90 percent of wheat varieties grown in Iran derive from CIMMYT selections or germplasm used in Iranian crossbreeding programs, and dozens of Iranian scientists have been trained at CIMMYT.

8. Nepal

1966-Current. Focus on Wheat, Maize

Currently active

CIMMYT has collaborated on diverse agricultural projects with the Nepal Agricultural Research Council (NARC), the Department of Agriculture, and with other partners in that country.

9. Pakistan

11. Turkey

1966-Current. Focus on Wheat, Maize

Currently active

CIMMYT has a long history of collaboration with Turkey beginning in the mid-1960s, when the import of 22,000 tons of Mexican wheat cultivars initiated Turkey’s green revolution. In

1969 the National Wheat Improvement Project was established to help design a wheat improvement and training program for Turkish scientists. Turkey also took part in the 1987 agreement

between ICARDA and CIMMYT to support the North African Wheat Programs which have increased production of that grain in West Asia, North Africa and Central Asia.
