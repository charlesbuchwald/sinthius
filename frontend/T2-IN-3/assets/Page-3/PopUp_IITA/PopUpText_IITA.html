The International Institute of Tropical Agriculture (Ibadan, Nigeria) founded in 1967, researches biotechnology and genetic improvement, natural resource management, plant production and plant health, and social science and agribusiness for key tropical food crops including banana and plantain, maize, cassava, soybean, cowpea, tree crops, and yam. It accounts for over 70 per cent of CGIAR impact in sub-Saharan Africa.
