The International Center for Agricultural Research in the Dry Areas, or ICARDA, (formerly in Aleppo, Syria, now in Rabat, Morocco) was founded in 1977. It promotes agricultural development and improved livelihoods in non-tropical dry areas of the developing world which are home to 2.5 billion people. Activities include crop improvement research, capacity building and training programs, and the maintenance of a germbank with 148,000 seed collections of unique landraces and wild relatives of cereals, legumes and forages collected from dryland areas worldwide.
